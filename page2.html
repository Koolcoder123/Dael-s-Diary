My Second Entry: Standing Up

Rhavaryn is calm; not much happens. And sometimes that's good. I haven't been doing much, just sitting around. Gathering my thoughts, standing back up, if you will. Well, if I will. It's my diary.


          It's still shocking that I had been locked in a unique world, one of lies. We lost the war; humanity lost the war. I don't want to get too much into the situation I was in; I regret so much killing my father, allowing myself to be swallowed by those thoughts and ideals. I guess I just needed a lie to rest in. Makes me wonder how many more lies make up this world. Here in Rhavaryn, there are plenty of chronicles of Lumenoran history. I've read so much. From ant people in a far-away desert, or a long-gone warrior who fought with the SFRL, who supplied them. I've heard he has many things, and those should be in the hands of Rhavaryn. I've read about a time when the Nether invaders hadn't entered this world, which I don't know how I feel about. Xadia did allow them passage, I've learned from people here, so I guess Netharia, as they are called, have let go of their chance to do something great and took advantage of the situation. I've read about another civilization by the ant people of the south, the Solmerites or Thurfirites; any name works, I guess. I'll visit them sometime to learn more. I care little regarding their religion, but about their society, for it's one which is still showing life.


          There's always a hope to become something big in history; I'll also plan to do that. In what way I'm not sure, probably just serving Rhavaryn. Why would I worry about the greater scale right now? There's so much right outside I should be working on; probably sometime soon I'll begin helping in Rhavaryn. And if the work seems too boring, I'll begin my world travel.


Anyway, Xadia will be with me.
